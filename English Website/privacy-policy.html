Privacy Policy

Last updated: October 28, 2021

This privacy policy will explain what information is collected when someone accesses and/or uses this website, the uses of that information, the way we secure and protect such information, who has access to the information, the location where your data is kept, and when the information is collected. You can view Namecheap's official privacy policy here, but this policy attempts to be more comprehensive and simplified.

What data is collected and why

The following data is collected by the hosting provider of this website and, according to the hosting provider, is not kept for more than 24 hours:

  • The IP address (Internet Protocol address) of the user/visitor.
  • The geolocation (based on the IP address) of the user/visitor.
  • The visit duration of how long the user/visitor has stay on the website.
  • The OS (operating system) of the user/visitor.
  • The browser type (browser) of the user/visitor.
  • The referrer URL (the website the user came from) of the user/visitor.

The data stated above is used and collected for the following purposes:

  • Understanding how visitors access the site to better optimise the website and services of the hosting provider.
  • Understanding how the site is performing (if we are seeing more or less visitors on average)
  • Diagnose and debug technical problems and errors.
  • Defending & protecting the website and services of the hosting provider from abuse.

When is yoru data collected and shared

When we collect your data:

The website administrator likes to adhere to a “the less we know the better” policy, meaning that we try to collect as little user/visitor information as possible. When you access this website, the hosting provider (Namecheap, Inc) will collect the data listed above. The New Oil does not have control over this collection process, and therefore cannot reduce or expand it. The New Oil highly encourages you to visit with a VPN or Tor browser to avoid this data collection. The data collected by the hosting provider is - to our knowledge - limited to your access and use of this website and does not use any tracking technologies such as cookies or beacons to track or identify your or your activity on other websites.

Who has access to your data:

Namecheap, Inc has access to all this data as this data is forcibly collected by the company and the company is not zero-knowledge.

The New Oil does have access to the information listed above, but typically is only interested in the number of unique visits. No member of The New Oil ever copies, screenshots, or in any other way records this data except in the annual transparency report (see "When is your data shared" below).

When is your data shared:

The administrator does not ever share data except total unique visitors each year as part of The New Oil's annual transparency report. The administrator is US-based, however, and will comply with any legally-binding orders from law enforcement or government agency for user data. However, given that the hosting provider collects this same data, law enforcement is much more likely to approach the provider rather than the administrator.

You can view Namecheap's policy about sharing information here.

Where is your data stored and how do we protect it

Where is your data stored:

Your data is stored with Namecheap, Inc which is a company based in the United States, which has data centres in the USA, EU, UK. We do not control those data centres or have a choice over which to use, and therefore do not have control over the security of your data. The specific locations of those data centres are as follows:

  • the European data centre is based in the Netherlands, Amsterdam.
  • The United Kingdom data centre is located in a data centre controlled by Timico Ltd close to Nottingham, in the Midlands.
  • The United States data centres are from Phoenix NAP, LLC located in Phoenix, Arizona, USA.

How do we protect your data:

As stated in the text above, “we do not control those data centres and there for do not have control over the security of your data“. However, The New Oil's Namecheap account is protected with a strong, unique password that exceeds current guidelines, software-token two-factor authentication, and a unique username not used anywhere else. The server itself is also hardened in accordance with recommendations from our technical adviser.

GDPR Compliance

EU citizens have the following rights under the GDPR:

  • The Right to Access. (Article 15 of the GDPR, link)
  • The Right to Rectification. (Article 16 of the GDPR, link)
  • The Right to Erasure. (Article 17 of the GDPR. link)
  • The Right to Restrict Processing. (Article 18 of the GDPR, link)
  • The Right to Data Portability. (Article 20 of the GDPR, link)
  • The Right to Object. (Article 21 of the GDPR, link)
  • The Right to Lodge a Complaint with a Supervisory Authority. (Article 77 of the GDPR, link)

The rights above are for European Union citizens. If you are not an EU citizen, please refer to your country's privacy laws and regulations for further help.

Modifications to this Privacy Policy

The New Oil reserves the right to change this Privacy Policy at any time. We will never knowingly reduce your rights under this Privacy Policy, and if we are aware of any addition to data collection or reduction of rights imposed by Namecheap, we will do our best to advertise a change to our privacy policy on Mastodon, Twitter, and Write.As. We cannot notify individuals of changes because we do not collect user contact information. We reserve the right to not publicly announce any minor changes to this policy, so for that reason we recommend you check this policy from time to time to see if any changes were made.
